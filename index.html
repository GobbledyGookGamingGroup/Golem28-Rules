Quick Reference

Mechanics & Sequence

Top-3 System: Roll the specified pool of dice and sum the highest three.

+1D Modifier: Add one extra die to the roll pool before selecting the top three.

Opposed Rolls: Both players roll simultaneously; subtract defender's sum from attacker's sum to find the difference.

Unopposed Rolls: Roll and compare the sum to the target Difficulty Class (DC).

Sequence of Play

  1. Start: Perform rituals to cast souls for golems, roll for initiative, and alternate deploying simulacrums.
  2. Action Phase: Starting with the initiative player, alternate taking actions with Animators and Followers until all action tokens are depleted.
  3. End Phase: Marks the end of the round. Resolve end-of-round abilities and effects.

Actions & Commands

Animators & Followers (1 AP Each)

  • ▶Move: Move in a straight line up to Nimbleness in inches.
  • ▶Attack: Make a physical attack against a target within range.
  • ▶Interact: Interact with a Base-to-Base (BtB) object or item.

Animator Only

  • ▶Command: Issue a purchased or free command to a single golem within command range.
  • ▶Command Chain: Issue multiple distinct (non-duplicate) commands in sequence to a single golem.
  • ▶Reanimate: Attempt to revive a downed golem within command range (Unopposed Mind, no DC specified, success restores 1d3 durability). Limit 1/bout per animator.

Free Golem Commands

Available to all golems. Purchased commands refer to unit cards.

  • Dash: Move in a straight line up to Nimbleness in inches.
  • Bash: Perform a physical attack at range.
  • Stash: Pick up an item, objective, or downed golem.

Impairments

  • STUNNEDCannot receive command chains.
  • ROTTINGDuring the end phase, flip a coin; on heads, take 1 damage.
  • GOUGEDThe golem suffers -1 to its Nimbleness.
  • DOWNEDPlaced on side; cannot receive any commands. Treated as Medium Terrain.
  • OoAOut of Action. Removed entirely from the play area.

Pushing & Collisions

Into Animators/Followers

Target may spend 1 AP to dodge (Unopposed Nimbleness DC 12+). Success = Move 3" out of path. If failed/not taken, make Opposed Ward Roll (Golem vs Target).

  • Golem Wins: Target takes 1 dmg. Golem stops.
  • Target Wins: Target takes 1d3 dmg, pushed back that many inches.

Into Golems

Both golems make an Opposed Ward roll.

  • Colliding Fails: Stops moving, takes 1d2 dmg.
  • Receiving Fails: Takes 1 dmg, pushed 1d3" along path.

Into Terrain

Colliding golem makes Unopposed Ward roll (DC 12+).

  • Success: Terrain takes 1d3 dmg.
  • Fail: Golem takes 1 dmg.

Terrain Rules

Obstacles (1" or less): Stops movement. Can be destroyed by Golem physical attack (Unopposed Clout DC 10+). Remove on success.

  • Difficult Terrain: (Rivers, debris, Downed Golems) Units lose 2" movement entering/leaving. Cannot be destroyed.
  • Dangerous Terrain: (Lava, ice) Moving through requires Unopposed Ward roll. Fail = 1 dmg. Cannot be destroyed.
  • Cover: If ranged physical attack path crosses terrain > 1", defender gets +1D to Ward.
  • Climbing: Golems cannot climb. Others can climb up to Nimbleness minus horizontal movement.
