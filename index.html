Game Primer

Golem28 is a tactical skirmish kitbasher wargame where players must carefully plan their actions, bet on successful outcomes, and destroy their enemies.

In this game, you do not just command an army; you build a "simulacrum" led by an Animator, supported by the terrifying golems they have brought to life, and accompanied by other followers. The game takes place in a dark fantasy world full of woes, tragedies, and misfortune. Ravaging the world are simulacra, which are groups of golems and followers led by animators. The animator is the true mastermind and the only unit capable of issuing commands to their golems, hulking monstrosities that possess no actions of their own.

At its core, Golem28 celebrates the hobbyist. Golems can be crafted and kitbashed from anything, old DnD minis, thrift store figurines, or literal trash. Animators and followers can be any 28mm minis you have from adjacent fantasy games.

So gather your simulacrum and go forth on your dark quest.

/// /// ///

1. Simulacrum

Your simulacrum is comprised of animators, golems, and followers. Your animator has created their golems to perform their bidding. And followers blindly follow their leader and simulacrum into battles.

1.1 Composition

Your first simulacrum consists of one of the two configurations: 1 animator, 1 golem, and 3 followers OR 1 animator, 2 golems, and 1 follower.

1.2 The Animator's Role

The leader of the simulacrum, animators vary wildly, from dark magii to chivalrous knights to manic madmen. As the mastermind of your warband, they are the only units that can command, as golems cannot act on their own.

1.3 The Nature of Golems

Golems are hulking monstrosities that have been animated to life. They are the constructs of the animators, brought into this world via insidious means. Think statues, constructs, or welded machinations. Because they lack their own will, they act at the direct command of their animator.

1.4 The Followers

Followers are the other individuals who accompany the animator. Mercenaries, questing soldiers, enslaved peasants, their backgrounds can vary greatly. While followers are much more frail than golems, they serve the simulacrum by marching forward, taking objectives, sneaking past enemies, or simply distracting the enemy.

1.5 The Story

Each simulacrum's story for venturing and conquest varies differently. When you create your simulacrum, you must give them a name and a reason as to why they are together.

2. The Animator

The leader of the simulacrum. They are the only units that can command golems as the constructs cannot act on their own. They also have individual actions such as movement, physical attacks, and interact.

2.1 Models and Base Sizes

The animator can be made from any fantasy era 28mm scale miniature. The grimmier, the better. They can be placed on 25-40mm bases.

2.2 Attributes

You build your animator by selecting values from 7 attributes. You have 15 worth to spend on attributes.

  • Command Range: How far an animator can command a golem.
  • Durability: The amount of damage an animator can take before going out of action (HP).
  • Nimbleness: How agile and quick the animator is (Speed).
  • Clout: How strong and physically destructive the animator is (Strength).
  • Physical Attack Range (PAR): How far the animator can make a physical attack. Base to Base (BtB) represents melee.
  • Ward: How physically tough and hardy the animator is (Defense).
  • Mind: How mentally adept the animator is and how well they can defend against magical attacks.

2.3 Actions

Animators typically receive 6 action points, and every action costs one point with the exception of command chains. These core actions are inherent and available to all animators.

  • Move: In a straight line, move up to Nimbleness in inches.
  • Attack: Make a physical attack at range against a declared target.
  • Interact: Interact with a BtB object or item.
  • Reanimate: Reanimate a downed golem within command range (Unopposed Mind roll). Recovers 1d3 durability. Once per bout per animator.
  • Command: Command a golem within command range. Action points wasted if the golem is outside of range.
  • Command Chains: A golem can receive multiple commands in a sequence. You must declare the sequence beforehand. Limited to one standard command per turn, no duplicates within a chain.

3. Golems

Unlike animators, golems do not have actions. They instead have commands which must be purchased like attributes. Golems have 21 Worth to purchase attributes and commands.

3.1 Models and Base Sizes

Golems are kit-bashed abominations placed on 40mm-120mm bases. Height gives no inherent bonuses, but base size matters for movement.

3.4 Commands

Animators issue commands to Golems. Commands must be purchased. There are three free commands: Bash, Dash, and Stash.

Movement

  • Dash (0W): Move up to nimbleness in inches.
  • Lash (1W): Roll 1d6 minus 3. Move that distance. If negative, pushed backward.

Violence

  • Bash (0W): Physical attack at range.
  • Crash (2W): Move 1d3" to target, physical attack +1D.
  • Slash (2W): Two physical attacks, -1D each.
  • Gnash (2W): Target within range makes unopposed ward save. Fail = enwrapped.
  • Gash (2W): Physical attack with -1D. On hit, target gouged.
  • Smash (1W): Physical attack +1D clout. Hit pushes target 1d3".
  • Thrash (2W): Physical attack all BtB units +1D clout.
  • Trash (1W): Physical attack BtB target -1D Clout. +1d3 dmg to terrain if hit.

Productive

  • Stash (0W): Pick up item/objective/downed golem.
  • Hash (1W): Unopposed Ward DC:12+ to recover 1 impairment.
  • Mishmash (1W): Unopposed Nimbleness to steal 1 item from BtB golem.

Magicka

  • Ash (2W): Mind roll +1D. If 10+, magically attack target within (R-9)" with 1D to mind.
  • Flash (2W): Mind roll. If 13+, magically attack target within (R-12)".
  • Rash (2W): Unopposed mind DC:12+. Target within (R-11)" is rotting.
  • Sash (2W): Unopposed mind DC:10+. Target within (R-10)" gets +1D to next roll.
  • Splash (2W): Unopposed mind DC:10+. Place 5" template within (R-9)". Units inside get -1D.
  • Whiplash (2W): Unopposed mind DC:12+. Push target 1d3" if within (R-11)".
  • Zash (1W): Unopposed mind DC:10+. Transfer 1d3 durability to target in LoS.
/// /// ///

4. The Followers

The followers are the grunts or fodder that follow the animator. While nowhere near as powerful as golems, followers are the doers and fodder of the simulacrum.

Follower attributes are exactly like animators except they cannot command golems. They have fewer action points per turn and each action costs one point.

  • Move: In a straight line, move up to Nimbleness in inches.
  • Attack: Make a physical attack at range.
  • Interact: Interact with a BtB object or item.

5. Mechanics

5.1 Dice Rolls

Rolls in Golem28 are made via a top-3 approach. Roll a specified number of dice (the pool) and sum the highest three dice. +1D means add one dice to the pool.

E.G. A golem has 4 clout and is making an attack with +1D. It rolls 5 dice and takes the sum of the top 3.

5.1.1 Opposed Rolls

Both players roll their pool at the same time and compare results. Look up the difference to determine the outcome. If the pool has only 1 or 2 dice, just sum what you have.

5.1.2 Unopposed Rolls

Success is determined if the roll result is equal to or greater than the specified Difficulty Class (DC).

5.2 Movement

Move in a straight line, edge of base to edge of base. Animators and followers can climb up to their nimbleness minus horizontal movement made. Golems cannot climb.

5.5 Impairments & Status

  • Stunned: Cannot chain commands.
  • Rotting: Flip a coin at maintenance phase. Heads = 1 damage.
  • Gouged: -2 to Nimbleness.
  • Downed: Cannot receive commands; model placed down.
  • Out of Action (OoA): Removed from play area.

5.6 Pushing and Collisions

  • Into Animators/Followers: May dodge (Action Point, Unopposed Nimble DC12+ to move 3"). If failed/not taken, opposed Ward. If animator/follower fails: takes 1d3 dmg, pushed back that many inches. Success: 1 dmg, golem stops.
  • Into Golems: Opposed Ward roll. Colliding fails: stops, 1d2 dmg. Receiving fails: 1 dmg, pushed 1d6" along path.
  • Into Terrain: Unopposed Ward DC12+. Success: terrain takes 1d3 dmg. Fail: Golem takes 1 dmg.

6. Playing the Game

1. Start: Players perform The Ritual to cast souls into their golems. Deploy forces.

2. Action Phase: Alternate activating animators using Action Points.

3. Soul Phase: Alternate resolving soul effects for golems.

4. End Phase: End of round effects occur.

6.6 Souls

Before bouts, animators perform rituals by casting souls into their bodies, pulled from the ethereum. Souls are categorized into 6 domains.

6.7 The Ritual

  1. Take 6d6 and drop (don't roll) them onto the table, at least 6" above the surface.
  2. You must stack any duplicates into towers. Choose one dice to be the base, then stack the others. Do not move the base dice.
  3. Take the remaining dice and drop them at least 6" above the surface (you can use this to knock over towers you don't want).
  4. Repeat stacking any new duplicates.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 one more time.
  6. Count the numbers and mark them down for your golem.

7.1 Grievous Greed

Setup: Do not place a piece of terrain in the middle of the board.

Animators have 6 action points and followers have 3 action points. 3 treasure tokens are placed on the area: 1 in the middle, 1 via scatter dice (1d8 inches away), 1 mirrored on the opposite side.

Animators/followers can interact to carry treasure (-1 Nimbleness). 4 Battle Rounds. Most units carrying treasure wins.

  • • +1D to the winner
  • • +1D for each side that downed a golem
  • • +2D for each side that downed an animator
  • • +1D for each treasure taken off board edge

7.2 King Among Kings

Setup: Place 1 objective marker in the middle of the board.

Animators have 6 action points and followers have 3 action points. 4 Battle Rounds. Most units within 3" of the objective wins.

  • • +1D to the winner
  • • +1D for each side that downed a golem
  • • +2D for each side that downed an animator

7.3 From Whence They Came

Animators have 6 action points and followers have 3 action points. Destroy as many enemies as possible.

4 Battle Rounds. Most units destroyed wins.

  • • +1D to the winner
  • • +2D for each side that downed a golem
  • • +3D for each side that downed an animator

7.4 Assault and Defense

Setup: Randomly decide attacker/defender. Large terrain in middle. Defender deploys around terrain (within 3"). Defender places all terrain. Attacker deploys any unit within any board edge.

Animators have 6 action points and followers have 3 action points. Attacker tries to take control of objective; defender thwarts.

4 Battle Rounds. Most units destroyed wins.

  • • +1D to the winner
  • • +2D for each side that downed a golem
  • • +3D for each side that downed an animator
